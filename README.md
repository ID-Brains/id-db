# Project Hikma 🏛️

> **"Knowledge is the only treasure that increases when shared."**

Welcome to **Project Hikma** (Arabic for *Wisdom*). This is more than just a repository; it is a digital commons, a collective memory for students, and a gateway to the world of Open Source. We are building a high-performance, community-driven knowledge base for academic notes, summaries, and resources.

---

## 🏺 Why "Project Hikma"?

The name is a tribute to **Bayt al-Hikma** (The House of Wisdom) of the Abbasid Golden Age in Baghdad.

During that era, Bayt al-Hikma served as a major intellectual hub where scholars from diverse backgrounds gathered to translate, preserve, and expand upon the world's knowledge. It was the epicenter of a massive movement that turned disparate fragments of information into a structured, accessible, and evolving body of science and philosophy.

We aim to revive that spirit in a modern, digital context. By centralizing student-led knowledge and making it open to all, we are building our own "House of Wisdom"-ensuring that the insights of today's students are not lost but are instead built upon by the next generation.

---

## 🌟 The Vision

In the traditional academic world, knowledge is often siloed in private notebooks, expiring group chats, or lost between semesters. **Project Hikma** changes that by treating academic content like code:
- **Versioned:** Track how notes evolve and improve over time.
- **Collaborative:** peer-reviewed by students and subject leads.
- **Accessible:** Static-first, lightning-fast, and RTL-friendly by default.
- **Permanent:** Built on zero-cost infrastructure to ensure it stays free forever.

## 🚀 Quick Guide

### 1. Find What You Need
The easiest way to consume this knowledge is through our live site (powered by Astro Starlight).

### 2. Share Your Wisdom
We believe technical barriers shouldn't stop great minds. You have two ways to contribute:

- **The Pro Way (Direct PR):** Fork the repo, add your Markdown files to `src/content/docs/`, and submit a Pull Request.
- **The Easy Way (Web Form) 'not yet':** Visit the `/contribute` page on our site. Authenticate with GitHub, fill the form, and we'll handle the Git magic for you. *Ideal for first-timers.*

### 3. Join the Discussion
Every page on our site is linked to **GitHub Discussions** via Giscus. If you have a question about a specific lecture or want to clarify a point, leave a comment directly on the page.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

We use a "Production-Grade" approach to static site generation:
- **Framework:** [Astro](https://astro.build/) + [Starlight](https://starlight.astro.build/) for a documentation-first UX.
- **Search:** [Pagefind](https://pagefind.app/) for serverless, multi-language search indexing.
- **Validation:** [Zod](https://zod.dev/) to enforce strict metadata (frontmatter) standards.
- **Styling:** [Tailwind CSS](https://tailwindcss.com/) for a modern, responsive interface.
- **Automation:** GitHub Actions for CI/CD, image optimization, and content extraction.

## 🎓 Our Style & Philosophy

We follow a **Popperian Epistemology**: We don't claim our notes are "The Absolute Truth." Instead, we treat them as *conjectures*-the best current understanding that is open to refinement and correction.

- **Human-Centric:** We write for students, not machines. Clear explanations over jargon.
- **Open-Source Culture:** We use this project to teach students Git, Markdown, and the ethics of collaboration.
- **Bilingual Support:** Full RTL support for Arabic content. Just set `language: "ar"` in your file's header.
