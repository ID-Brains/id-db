# Our Philosophy

> "Knowledge is the only treasure that increases when shared."

The **College Knowledge Base** (id-db) isn't just a technical repository; it's a digital commons built by students, for students. Our approach is rooted in the belief that academic success should be accessible, collaborative, and community-driven.

## Core Values

### 1. Radical Accessibility
We believe that technical barriers should never prevent someone from sharing knowledge. Whether you are a Git power-user or someone who has never heard of a "Pull Request," your contribution is valuable. This is why we prioritize a **Web Form Flow** alongside traditional Git workflows as a starter but our goal here is to get you into opensource culture.

### 2. Zero-Cost Sustainability
Knowledge should be free, and so should the platform that hosts it. We architect our system to run entirely on free, open-source infrastructure (GitHub Pages, Actions, and Discussions). This ensures the project can live as long as the community needs it, without the burden of hosting costs.

### 3. Popperian Epistemology: Growth via Refinement
Following Karl Popper’s theory of knowledge, we view academic notes not as static "truth," but as **conjectures**. Knowledge grows through the process of **critical preference and falsification**. Every Pull Request is an opportunity to refine, correct, and improve upon previous versions. We embrace "error" as a necessary step toward better understanding as most of our generation needs to have the inner piece with being wrong sometimes this days.

### 4. Open-Source as Education
We view this project as a "Gateway to Open Source." By contributing, students don't just share notes-they learn the **Open Source Culture**: transparency, peer review, and collective ownership. We introduce them to professional tools like Markdown, Git, and CI/CD in a high-impact environment so in later stages they are convinent with the environment.

### 5. Content over Container
We focus on the content. Our schema (Zod/YAML) is designed to make data structured and machine-readable, but the end goal is always a better reading and learning experience for the student as we will work to evolve the UX overtime.
